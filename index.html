Short Bio

Rodrigo Laigner is a first-year PhD student in the Department of Computer Science at University of Copenhagen .

He holds a Master's in Informatics from PUC-Rio (2020). Rodrigo holds a Bachelor's degree in Information Systems from the Fluminense Federal University (UFF), Brazil (2017), in cooperation with University of Colorado Denver (UCD), USA (2014-2015).

He is a research scholar in data management systems for Data Management Systems Group at University of Copenhagen since 2020 under the supervision of Prof. Dr. Yongluan Zhou and Prof. Dr. Marcos Antonio Vaz Salles which is part of the Software, Data, People & Society Research Section from the Department of Computer Science at University of Copenhagen. His main research interests are big data systems, big data software engineering, database systems, and software architecture. His researches are available in publications.

He has worked extensively in industry for projects in software engineering and data management systems, mostly related to web-based systems, optimization of access patterns from ORM source code, database migration, and tuning. Furthermore, has worked for research projects in software engineering, information systems, and data systems. Relevant venues have accepted his work for publication, such as the EuroMicro Conference on Software Engineering and Advanced Applications (SEAA), Brazilian Symposium on Software Engineering (SBES), and Brazilian Symposium on Databases (SBBD). Rodrigo collaborates with researchers in Brazil, such as Dr. Marcos Kalinowski (PUC-Rio). He has received the third best paper award from the Brazilian Symposium on Software Engineering (SBES 2019), the main Brazilian venue in software engineering.

Feel free to contact me. Specially if you are a researcher searching for collaboration.
