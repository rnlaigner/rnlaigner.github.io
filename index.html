Short Bio

Rodrigo Laigner is a third-year PhD fellow employed in the Department of Computer Science at University of Copenhagen under the supervision of Prof. Yongluan Zhou. Previously, has been co-adivsed by Marcos Antonio Vaz Salles.

His research targets building efficient and consistent database systems for data management requirements found in new approaches for developing distributed applications (with substantial data management logic at the application tier), such as those following the microservice archietctural style.

If you are a graduate student from the Master of Science (MSc) in Computer Science program at the University of Copenhagen and have an interest in pursuing a thesis in topics related to event-driven architecture, microservices, and big data systems, do not hesitate to contact me.

Given time constraints related to my research project, I am no longer accepting students. However, if you are highly motivated, independent, has good grades in Advanced Computer Systems, is taking the Big Data Systems course next block, and is interested in working with data-intensive systems, drop me an email.
